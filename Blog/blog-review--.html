Masamune-kun’s Revenge R Anime Season Delayed by 3 Months to July Due to COVID-19







Masamune-kun’s Revenge, a beloved romantic comedy anime series adapted from Hazuki Takeoka and Tiv's manga of the same name, has faced an unforeseen setback. Originally slated for an April 2023 release, the much-anticipated second season, Masamune-kun’s Revenge R, has been postponed by three months due to the persistent challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. Fans who have anxiously awaited this sequel since the first season's 2017 premiere were understandably disappointed by the news. Nonetheless, this delay is not without precedent, as the pandemic has disrupted countless projects across the entertainment industry, leading to postponements and cancellations. The decision to defer the release of Masamune-kun’s Revenge R stems from a commitment to the safety and well-being of the show's cast and crew. Additionally, it reflects Silver Link's dedication to upholding the series' renowned quality. The production team is steadfast in their mission to deliver an exceptional product that not only meets fan expectations but also prioritizes the health and safety of all involved in the creative process.

Despite this setback, fans can still eagerly anticipate the return of their favorite characters and the unfolding of the story. Masamune-kun’s Revenge R follows the journey of Masamune Makabe, a high school student who previously suffered teasing and rejection from Aki Adagaki. Seeking vengeance, Masamune undergoes a remarkable transformation, becoming a handsome and popular student with the aim of winning Aki's affection. However, he soon discovers that matters of the heart are far from straightforward.
