Masamune Makabe is still working to exact revenge after the turbulent cultural festival by making the "Cruel Princess" Aki Adagaki fall madly in love with him before breaking it off.





Masamune gets the ideal chance to avenge his early loss since his class is travelling to Paris, also regarded as the City of Love. The two meet Muriel Besson, a French high school otaku who hopes to write a romantic comedy manga series, before Masamune has a chance to impress Aki. Masamune is asked for help since Muriel thinks he would make a great role model for the protagonist. The boy drags Aki along to serve as inspiration for the love interest's personality before reluctantly agreeing. But first, the two have to demonstrate to Muriel what true Japanese love is like. To further complicate matters, Kanetsugu Gasou is tricking and abusing Aki by posing as her childhood buddy Masamune. Masamune's retribution is turning out to be a very challenging undertaking because of misunderstandings and love rivals galore. Masamune Makabe previously suffered terribly at the hands of a rich and lovely girl named Aki Adagaki, who called him "Piggy" because of his fat appearance. Masamune returns as a stunningly attractive, if selfish, high school student as he seeks retribution against his tormentor. He is ready to seek revenge when he runs into Aki again. Masamune slowly starts to get to know Aki with the help of the wealthy girl's maid, Yoshino Koiwai, with the intention of breaking her heart when the moment is right. Masamune, however, starts to doubt the goals of his cunning plans and whether carrying them out is what his heart truly wishes as his friendship with Aki deepens.

My Spacial Review On Masamune-kun no Revenge


No Spoiler Review

The primary complaint I've heard about this series is that it lacks a satisfactory conclusion. For my part, rather than stressing out about the lack of a sequel, I just bought and read the manga, which ends at book number 10. I think this is a very well-done romantic comedy; the characters' interactions are not insignificant, and once you start watching, you can't stop. The characters are generally not very smart, but keep in mind that this is a rom-com and not a drama or opera. Yoshino will undoubtedly stand out as the most gorgeous character among all of them.


Reviews: on Rewatch 1

Yes, the series doesn't end (it goes to Manga number 6), but this didn't bother me because I don't think all series need to have a definitive conclusion. There is only one thing that struck me as odd: Masamune occasionally uses unique, made-up sentences with girls, and it's unclear to me from the author's point of view whether these sentences are considered "good" or "bad" because, at times, they are not actually good sentences, though I have the impression that the author may believe they are. Although I have lived in the UK and the US and am neither Japanese nor American, I don't believe a girl would appreciate them all.
